1. **`/l/` is the primary PLP pattern** — all top-level category listing pages use `/l/{category}/`, making this an easy signal to identify PLPs site-wide.
2. **Auto's and electronics are the dominant categories** — they score highest due to strong Google organic presence, reflecting high-intent consumer demand in Belgium.
3. **JavaScript-rendered listings** — product counts could not be extracted from HTML; the site relies on client-side rendering for listing results.
4. **`/lp/` pages are SEO landing pages** — these are secondary listing entry points targeting specific search queries (e.g. `/lp/fietsen/`) and score well on Google despite not being primary nav categories.
5. **All 19 nav-linked categories score identically on structural signals** — differentiation at the top level comes entirely from Google prominence.
