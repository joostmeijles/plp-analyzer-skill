## Key Observations

1. **The platform uses a depth-2 category structure for main PLPs.** The most product-rich and Google-visible pages are at depth 2 (e.g., `/fietsonderdelen/fietsbanden`, `/fietskleding-heren/fietskleding`). Depth-1 pages like `/fietsonderdelen` act as gateway landing pages with subcategory navigation rather than full product grids.

2. **Pagination caps at 100 products per page**, making the 100-product pages a lower bound — actual category sizes are likely much larger. The `/outlet` page (100+) and the clothing/shoes/components categories demonstrate the broadest assortments.

3. **FuturumShop maintains a very large vendor brand directory** (280+ brand pages in vendors.xml such as `/shimano`, `/garmin`, `/specialized`), which function as brand-filtered PLPs. These are significant for SEO but were excluded from the main scoring as they are brand pages rather than editorial categories.

4. **The outlet page (`/outlet`) ranks #1 by combined score** due to its depth-1 status (+20), 100+ visible products, nav-linked prominence, and confirmed Google indexing. It is likely the highest-traffic cross-category PLP.

5. **Google search visibility confirms the cycling clothing and components categories** (`/fietskleding-heren`, `/fietsonderdelen/fietsbanden`, `/fietsonderdelen/fietswielen`) as the primary organic traffic drivers, consistent with the site's core identity as a specialist cycling retailer.
